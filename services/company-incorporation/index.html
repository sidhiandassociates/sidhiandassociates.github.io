Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to register a Private Limited Company in India?
Private Limited Company registration costs ‚Çπ14,999 (all-inclusive with government fees, stamp duty, and professional charges). This includes name approval, MOA/AOA drafting, DSC for 2 directors, DIN, SPICe+ filing, incorporation certificate, PAN, TAN, and first year compliance support. There are absolutely no hidden charges.
How long does company registration take?
Company registration typically takes 7-10 working days from document submission. Name approval takes 1-2 days via RUN form, DSC issuance takes 2-3 days, and incorporation certificate is issued within 5-7 days after SPICe+ filing. We expedite the entire process to ensure the quickest possible registration.
What documents are required for company registration?
Required documents include: PAN card, Aadhaar card, and passport size photos of all directors/partners, address proof (voter ID, passport, driving license), proof of registered office (rental agreement min 11 months or ownership documents), utility bills (electricity/water) not older than 2 months, NOC from property owner, and bank statement of directors. We provide a complete checklist and help prepare all documentation.
What is the difference between Private Limited and LLP?
Private Limited Company has shareholders and directors with limited liability, requires mandatory audit annually, has higher compliance requirements, and is better for raising funding. LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) has partners with limited liability, requires audit only if turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs, has lower compliance requirements, and is more suitable for professional services. Private Limited is best for startups planning to raise investment, while LLP is ideal for consultants, CAs, lawyers, and small service businesses.
Can foreigners register a company in India?
Yes, foreigners can register companies in India. However, at least one director must be an Indian resident. Foreign nationals need additional documents including passport, visa, proof of foreign address, and notarized/apostilled documents. We handle all formalities for foreign company registration including FEMA compliance, RBI approvals, and FDI norms. Foreign investment up to 100% is allowed in most sectors (except a few restricted areas).
What is the minimum capital requirement for Private Limited Company?
There is NO minimum capital requirement for Private Limited Company registration in India. You can start with even ‚Çπ1,000 as authorized capital. However, we typically recommend keeping authorized capital between ‚Çπ1-5 lakhs depending on your business plans. The stamp duty is calculated on authorized capital (0.3-0.7% varying by state), so keeping it reasonable helps reduce initial costs.
Do I need a physical office to register a company?
Yes, you need a registered office address in India to register a company. It can be your home, rented office, or coworking space. You need proof of address (rental agreement min 11 months or ownership documents), utility bill, and NOC from the property owner. The registered office address will appear on the incorporation certificate. You can also use our virtual office services if needed.
What is DSC and DIN? Are they mandatory?
DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) is an electronic signature used to file documents with MCA. DIN (Director Identification Number) is a unique identification number for directors. Both are mandatory for company registration. DSC costs are included in our package. DIN is issued free by MCA. DSC is valid for 2 years and needs renewal. We handle the entire DSC and DIN application process.
What are the annual compliance requirements after incorporation?
After incorporation, you need to: (1) File annual ROC returns (AOC-4 and MGT-7), (2) Conduct minimum 4 board meetings per year, (3) Hold Annual General Meeting (AGM), (4) File income tax returns, (5) Maintain statutory registers and minutes books, (6) Get accounts audited annually. We provide first year compliance support free and can handle all ongoing compliances at reasonable annual charges.
Can I convert my proprietorship/partnership to Private Limited later?
Yes, you can convert proprietorship or partnership firm to Private Limited Company later. The process involves: (1) Registering new Private Limited Company, (2) Transferring all assets and liabilities, (3) Obtaining NOCs from creditors, (4) Closing old entity, and (5) Updating all registrations (GST, bank account, etc.). We handle the entire conversion process including valuation, transfer documentation, and compliance filings.
