What is Income Tax Return (ITR)?

An Income Tax Return is a form filed with the Income Tax Department disclosing income earned, taxes paid, and tax refunds claimed for a financial year

Important ITR Filing Deadlines

For Individuals (Non-Audit)

Deadline: 31st July

Applies to salaried individuals, pensioners, and those with income from house property, capital gains, other sources without business requiring audit.

Late Filing Penalty:
‚Ä¢ ‚Çπ5,000 if filed after July 31
‚Ä¢ ‚Çπ1,000 if total income below ‚Çπ5 lakhs
‚Ä¢ Can file belated return till 31st December

For Businesses (Audit Cases)

Deadline: 31st October

Applies to businesses and professionals whose accounts require audit under Income Tax Act or other laws.

Late Filing Penalty:
‚Ä¢ ‚Çπ5,000 if filed after October 31
‚Ä¢ Interest on unpaid tax liability
‚Ä¢ Can file belated return till 31st December

Revised Return

Deadline: 31st December

If you discover an error in your filed ITR, you can file a revised return before the end of assessment year or before completion of assessment, whichever is earlier.

When to Revise:
‚Ä¢ Wrong income reported
‚Ä¢ Missed deductions/exemptions
‚Ä¢ Calculation errors
‚Ä¢ Wrong ITR form used

Belated Return

Deadline: 31st December

If you missed the original deadline, you can file belated return with late fee penalty till 31st December of assessment year.

Consequences:
‚Ä¢ Late fee applicable
‚Ä¢ Cannot carry forward losses
‚Ä¢ Interest on unpaid tax

How long does ITR filing take?
We complete ITR filing within 24-48 hours for most cases. Simple salaried returns (ITR-1) with Form 16 are typically filed same day or next day. Returns with capital gains, multiple income sources (ITR-2) take 1-2 days. Business returns (ITR-3) may take 2-3 days depending on complexity. What documents do I need for ITR filing?
Required documents include: Form 16 (for salaried), bank statements (all accounts), interest certificates, Form 26AS/AIS, capital gains statements, investment proofs (80C, 80D, 80G), rent receipts (for HRA), home loan certificates, PAN card, and Aadhaar card. For business income: books of accounts, GST returns, sales/purchase records, P&L, and balance sheet. You can share documents via WhatsApp, email, or upload on our portal.
Can I get maximum tax refund?
Yes, our expert CAs ensure you claim all eligible deductions and exemptions to maximize your tax refund. We review your investments under Section 80C (PPF, ELSS, LIC), 80D (health insurance), 80G (donations), 24(b) (home loan interest), HRA exemption, standard deduction, and other applicable sections. We also optimize between old and new tax regime to minimize your tax liability and maximize refund.
What is the ITR filing deadline?
ITR filing deadline for individuals (non-audit cases) is 31st July of the assessment year. For businesses requiring audit, the deadline is 31st October. If you miss the deadline, you can file belated return till 31st December with late fee penalty of ‚Çπ5,000 (‚Çπ1,000 if income below ‚Çπ5 lakhs). However, belated filing means you cannot carry forward certain losses. It's always best to file before 31st July.
Which ITR form should I file?
ITR form depends on your income sources: ITR-1 for salaried individuals with income up to ‚Çπ50 lakhs and one house property; ITR-2 for individuals with capital gains, multiple properties, or foreign income; ITR-3 for business/professional income; ITR-4 for presumptive income (44AD/44ADA); ITR-5 for firms and LLPs; ITR-6 for companies. Our CAs will determine the correct form after reviewing your income sources and provide guidance.
Is ITR filing mandatory?
ITR filing is mandatory if: your total income exceeds basic exemption limit (‚Çπ2.5 lakhs for individuals, ‚Çπ3 lakhs for senior citizens, ‚Çπ5 lakhs for super senior citizens); you have deposited ‚Çπ1 crore or more in bank accounts; paid ‚Çπ2 lakhs or more for foreign travel; electricity bill exceeds ‚Çπ1 lakh; or you want to claim tax refund. Even if not mandatory, filing ITR is beneficial for loan applications, visa processing, and carrying forward losses.
What is ITR-V and how to verify it?
ITR-V (Income Tax Return Verification) is an acknowledgement slip generated after filing ITR. You must verify ITR-V within 30 days through: (1) Aadhaar OTP - instant verification, (2) Net Banking through your bank, (3) Demat account verification, (4) Bank account verification, or (5) Sending signed physical ITR-V to CPC Bangalore by speed post. E-verification through Aadhaar OTP is fastest and most convenient method. Without verification, your return is considered not filed.
How to track my tax refund?
You can track tax refund status on income tax e-filing portal. After ITR verification, processing takes 1-3 months. Refund is directly credited to your bank account linked with PAN. You can check status at: (1) Income Tax Portal - login and check refund status, (2) NSDL website using PAN and assessment year, (3) Tin NSDL refund status page, or (4) Call income tax helpline 1800-180-1961. Ensure your bank account is pre-validated on IT portal for faster refund credit.
Can you help with income tax notices?

Our experienced CAs analyze the notice, prepare detailed responses with supporting documents, represent you before income tax authorities, and handle the complete compliance process. We also assist with appeals, rectification applications, and penalty proceedings if required. Contact us immediately if you receive any IT notice.
