LLP Agreement Stamp Duty in India

Complete Guide to State-Wise Rates, Calculation Methods & Payment Process for 2025

What is LLP Agreement Stamp Duty?

Stamp duty on an LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) Agreement is a mandatory tax levied by state governments to make the document legally valid and enforceable. This government tax transforms your LLP Agreement from a simple piece of paper into a legally binding document that can be used as evidence in a court of law.

Important: An unstamped or insufficiently stamped LLP Agreement cannot be admitted as evidence in court. If a dispute arises between partners, the judge will refuse to acknowledge an improperly stamped agreement.

Why is Stamp Duty Important?

  • Legal Validity: Makes your LLP Agreement legally enforceable and admissible in court
  • Compliance: Fulfills mandatory legal requirements under state stamp acts
  • Protection: Safeguards your business relationships and partner rights
  • Government Recognition: Provides official validation of your LLP structure
  • Dispute Resolution: Essential for resolving conflicts between partners

💡 Key Point: Stamp duty is a state subject in India, meaning rates vary significantly from one state to another based on where your LLP's registered office is located.

Understanding LLP Agreement

An LLP Agreement is a written contract between the partners of a Limited Liability Partnership or between the LLP and its partners. This document serves as the "constitution" or "rulebook" of your business.

Key Components of LLP Agreement:

  • Partner Rights & Duties: Defines roles and responsibilities of all partners
  • Capital Contribution: Specifies how much each partner invests
  • Profit Sharing: Outlines how profits and losses are distributed
  • Decision Making: Establishes procedures for business decisions
  • Partner Entry/Exit: Rules for adding new partners or handling exits
  • Designated Partners: Identifies roles of designated partners
  • Business Objectives: States the purpose and goals of the LLP

⚠️ Compliance Deadline: The LLP Agreement must be filed with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) within 30 days of LLP incorporation using LLP Form 3. Failure to file on time results in a penalty of ₹100 per day.

How is Stamp Duty Calculated?

Stamp duty calculation varies by state, but most follow one of these three common methods:

1. Fixed Amount Method

Some states charge a flat, fixed fee regardless of capital contribution. This is the simplest method, typically ranging from ₹100 to ₹1,000.

2. Percentage of Capital Contribution

Most states calculate stamp duty as a percentage of the total capital contributed by all partners. Common rates range from 0.1% to 1% of capital.

3. Slab-Based System

Several states use a tiered system based on capital contribution ranges:

  • Up to ₹50,000: Lower rate (e.g., ₹50)
  • ₹50,000 to ₹2,00,000: Medium rate (e.g., ₹100)
  • Above ₹2,00,000: Higher rate (e.g., ₹200)
Factors Affecting Stamp Duty:
  1. State of Registration: Where your LLP's registered office is located
  2. Capital Contribution: Total investment by all partners
  3. Type of Agreement: Fresh incorporation vs. amendment
  4. Caps & Minimums: Many states have floor and ceiling limits

How to Pay Stamp Duty on LLP Agreement

There are two primary methods for paying stamp duty on your LLP Agreement:

1. Physical Mode (Non-Judicial Stamp Paper)

1

Visit Authorized Vendor

Go to an authorized stamp vendor or licensed stamp paper seller in your state. You can find vendors at district courts, sub-registrar offices, or designated outlets.

2

Purchase Stamp Paper

Buy non-judicial stamp paper of the appropriate value based on your state's stamp duty rate. Provide details like partner names and LLP name.

3

Execute Agreement

Print the LLP Agreement on the purchased stamp paper. Ensure all partners sign the agreement on the same day.

4

Notarization

Get the agreement notarized by a public notary for additional legal validation (highly recommended but not always mandatory).

2. Online Mode (E-Stamping)

E-stamping is becoming the preferred method in many states due to its speed, transparency, and convenience.

✅ E-Stamping Benefits: Faster processing, no risk of fake stamp papers, tamper-proof certificates, online payment options, and immediate generation of stamp certificate.

1

Visit E-Stamping Portal

Go to your state's official e-stamping website or the Stock Holding Corporation of India Limited (SHCIL) portal. States like Delhi, Karnataka, Gujarat, and Chandigarh offer e-stamping.

2

Register & Fill Details

Create an account or visit an authorized collection center. Fill in details about your LLP, partners, and capital contribution.

3

Make Payment

Pay the stamp duty online via net banking, credit/debit card, or at designated banks. Some states accept cash and demand drafts at collection centers.

4

Receive E-Stamp Certificate

Get a unique, tamper-proof e-stamp certificate with a unique identification number. Print this certificate and attach it to your LLP Agreement.

3. Franking Method

Some states allow franking of the LLP Agreement through authorized banks. Visit a designated bank branch, pay the stamp duty, and get the agreement franked with an official seal.

Important: Stamp duty cannot be paid directly through the MCA portal. You must follow your state's stamping procedure (physical, e-stamping, or franking) before filing the agreement with MCA.

Filing LLP Agreement with MCA

After paying stamp duty and executing the agreement, you must file it with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs:

1

Prepare Documents

Ensure the LLP Agreement is properly stamped, signed by all partners, and notarized (if applicable).

2

File LLP Form 3

Log into the MCA portal and file LLP Form 3 (Limited Liability Partnership Agreement) with the stamped agreement attached.

3

Meet Deadline

File within 30 days of receiving the Certificate of Incorporation. Late filing attracts a penalty of ₹100 per day.

4

Receive Confirmation

Once accepted, the MCA will update the LLP's records, and your agreement becomes part of the official documents.

Consequences of Not Paying Stamp Duty

Failing to pay appropriate stamp duty or underpaying can lead to serious legal and financial consequences:

  • Legal Invalidity: Agreement cannot be admitted as evidence in court
  • Heavy Penalties: Authorities can impose penalties up to 10 times the stamp duty amount due
  • Interest Charges: Accumulating interest on unpaid stamp duty
  • Registration Delays: MCA may reject or delay acceptance of your LLP Agreement
  • Audit Issues: Problems during compliance audits or due diligence
  • Business Complications: Difficulties in resolving partner disputes or enforcing rights
  • Funding Challenges: Investors and banks may hesitate if documents aren't properly stamped
Prevention is Cheaper: Paying the correct stamp duty upfront is always more cost-effective than dealing with penalties, interest, and legal complications later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Can I pay stamp duty for LLP Agreement online or through MCA Portal?
No, stamp duty cannot be paid directly on the MCA portal. You must pay through your state's authorized channels - either by purchasing stamp paper, using e-stamping facilities, or through bank franking. After payment, you can file the stamped agreement with MCA.
Q2. What happens if my LLP operates in multiple states?
Stamp duty is paid based on where your LLP's registered office is located, not where you conduct business. If your registered office is in Delhi, you pay Delhi stamp duty rates, even if you operate across multiple states.
Q3. Is stamp duty refundable if I make a mistake?
Stamp duty is generally non-refundable. However, under Section 54 of the Indian Stamp Act, 1899, you may claim a refund for unused, unspoilt stamp paper within 6 months of purchase, subject to a 10 paise per rupee deduction.
Q4. How long is stamp paper valid?
Under the Central Indian Stamp Act, there's no specific expiry date. However, states like Maharashtra require stamp paper to be used within 6 months of purchase. It's best practice to purchase stamp paper shortly before executing your agreement.
Q5. Do I need separate stamp duty for each partner's copy?
No, you only need to pay stamp duty once on the original agreement. You can make photocopies for distribution among partners without additional stamp duty.
Q6. What if there's no specific LLP stamp duty mentioned in my state's Stamp Act?
In states without specific LLP provisions, stamp duty is typically charged according to the rates applicable to partnership deeds as per the Finance Bill, 2009.
Q7. Is notarization of the LLP Agreement mandatory?
While not always legally mandatory, notarization is highly recommended. It provides additional legal validation and can be crucial if you need to present the agreement in court.
Q8. Can I use the same stamp paper if partners are from different states?
Yes, stamp duty is determined by the state where your LLP's registered office is located, regardless of where the partners reside. Use the stamp duty rate of the registration state.
Q9. Is stamp duty applicable when converting a company to LLP?
Yes, when converting a company to an LLP, the new LLP Agreement is treated as a fresh legal instrument requiring full stamp duty based on the capital structure and state rules.
Q10. What is the penalty for late filing of LLP Agreement?
If you fail to file the LLP Agreement with MCA within 30 days of incorporation, you'll incur a penalty of ₹100 per day of delay. This penalty is separate from stamp duty issues.
