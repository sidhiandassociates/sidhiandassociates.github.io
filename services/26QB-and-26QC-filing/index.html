Understanding Form 141 — Formerly 26QB & 26QC

Know when and why you need to file for property transactions and rent payments, and what changed under the Income-tax Act, 2025

What changed: Effective 1 April 2026, the Income-tax Act, 2025 merged Forms 26QB, 26QC, 26QD and 26QE into a single challan-cum-statement, Form No. 141. Property TDS (previously Form 26QB, Section 194-IA) is now Schedule B of Form 141, filed under Section 393(1). Rent TDS (previously Form 26QC, Section 194-IB) is now Schedule A. The TDS certificate issued to the seller/landlord is now Form No. 132, replacing Form 16B/16C. Compliance requirements, thresholds, and deadlines remain the same — only the form number, schedule structure, and section reference have changed.

🏠
Form 141 · Schedule B (formerly Form 26QB)

Property Sale TDS

When Required: When buying immovable property (land, building, house) valued at ₹50 lakhs or more.

TDS Rate:

  • 1% if seller is Resident Indian
  • 20% if seller is NRI
  • 20% if seller doesn't have PAN

Filing Deadline: Within 30 days from the end of the month in which such payment or registration is done

🏢
Form 141 · Schedule A (formerly Form 26QC)

Rent TDS

When Required: When paying rent exceeding ₹50,000 per month (₹6 lakhs annually) to an Indian resident.

TDS Rate:

  • 10% on rent payments (for resident)
  • 20% without PAN of landlord

Filing Deadline: Within 30 days of the end of the month in which TDS was deducted

⚠️ Important: Why Timely Filing is Critical

Penalties for Non-Filing:

  • Late filing fee: ₹200 per day (minimum ₹10,000, maximum equal to TDS amount)
  • Interest: 1.5% per month on TDS amount from due date till payment
  • Property registration may be refused without the challan
  • Seller cannot claim TDS credit in their ITR without proper filing

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the new name of Form 26QB and Form 26QC? +
Effective 1 April 2026, Forms 26QB, 26QC, 26QD and 26QE have been merged into a single unified challan-cum-statement called Form No. 141 under the Income-tax Act, 2025. Property TDS (formerly Form 26QB) is now Schedule B of Form 141, and rent TDS (formerly Form 26QC) is now Schedule A. The governing section changed from 194-IA/194-IB to Section 393(1), and the TDS certificate is now Form No. 132 instead of Form 16B/16C.
What is Form 141 Schedule B (formerly Form 26QB) and when is it required? +
Form 141 Schedule B is used to deposit TDS on property sale transactions. It's mandatory when you're buying immovable property (land, building, house, plot) valued at ₹50 lakhs or more. As a buyer, you must deduct TDS at 1% (for resident seller) or 20% (for NRI seller or seller without PAN) and file within 30 days from the end of the month in which the payment or property registration is done. This is filed online on the income tax portal after paying the TDS amount via challan.
What is Form 141 Schedule A (formerly Form 26QC) and who needs to file it? +
Form 141 Schedule A is for TDS deduction on rent payments. If you're a tenant (individual or business) paying rent exceeding ₹50,000 per month (or ₹6 lakhs annually) to a resident Indian landlord, you must deduct 10% TDS and file it. This applies to all types of rent — residential, commercial, or land. It must be filed quarterly within 30 days of the quarter end. Not applicable when paying rent to NRI landlords (different provisions apply).
How much does Form 141 (26QB/26QC) filing cost? +
Form 141 Schedule B (property) filing costs ₹2,000 (regular) or ₹3,000 (same-day priority service). Form 141 Schedule A (rent) filing costs ₹1,500 per quarter. Cancellation of a filed 26QB/Form 141 Schedule B starts from ₹2,500. These fees include TDS calculation, challan generation support, online filing, acknowledgement download, and the Form 132 TDS certificate.
Can Form 26QB (now Form 141 Schedule B) be cancelled after filing? +
Yes. If a property deal falls through, or a 26QB/Form 141 Schedule B was filed with incorrect details, we offer a cancellation and correction filing service starting from ₹2,500. This covers correction statement filing on the portal and refund claim guidance where applicable.
What happens if I don't file Form 141 (26QB/26QC) on time? +
Late filing attracts penalties: ₹200 per day late fee (minimum ₹10,000, maximum equal to TDS amount) plus interest at 1.5% per month on the TDS amount from due date. For property transactions, Sub-Registrar offices may refuse registration without the challan. The seller/landlord cannot claim TDS credit in their ITR without proper filing, which may lead to disputes.
How long does the filing process take? +
With all documents ready, we can complete the filing within 24 hours (regular service) or 4–6 hours (priority same-day service). The process involves: TDS calculation, challan payment by you, form preparation and filing by us, and Form 132 certificate download, available immediately after successful filing.
What documents are required for Form 141 Schedule B (26QB) filing? +
You need: 1) Buyer's PAN and Aadhaar, 2) Seller's PAN and Aadhaar, 3) Property details (address, survey number), 4) Sale deed or agreement copy, 5) Sale consideration amount, 6) Property registration details (if done), 7) TDS challan payment proof with BSR code. If the seller is NRI, you'll also need their passport copy and foreign address. We provide a detailed checklist based on your specific transaction.
Can I claim TDS refund if excess TDS was deducted? +
Yes, the seller/landlord can claim TDS refund while filing their income tax return. The TDS deducted appears in their AIS/Form 168 and can be claimed as tax credit. For property sales, if the seller's capital gains tax is lower than the 1% TDS deducted, they can claim the balance. We assist in downloading the Form 132 certificate which the seller needs to attach with their ITR.
Is Form 141 Schedule B (26QB) required for property below ₹50 lakhs? +
No, it is not required if the property value is below ₹50 lakhs. This threshold applies to the total sale consideration, not the stamp duty value. If you're buying multiple properties from the same seller in the same financial year and the total exceeds ₹50 lakhs, TDS is applicable on the combined value. Agricultural land is exempt regardless of value. For properties eligible for capital gains exemption, you may apply for a lower/nil TDS certificate.
Can I correct mistakes in an already filed Form 141 (26QB/26QC)? +
Yes, you can file a correction statement to rectify errors. Common corrections include: wrong PAN of buyer/seller/landlord, incorrect property address, TDS amount errors, or wrong payment dates. We charge ₹1,000 for correction/revision filing, and ₹2,500 onwards if the transaction itself needs to be cancelled. It's important to correct errors quickly, as wrong PAN or amount can prevent the seller/landlord from claiming TDS credit.
