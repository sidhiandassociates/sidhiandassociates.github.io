Frequently Asked Questions

What is Form 26QB and when is it required? +
Form 26QB is used to deposit TDS on property sale transactions. It's mandatory when you're buying immovable property (land, building, house, plot) valued at ‚Çπ50 lakhs or more. As a buyer, you must deduct TDS at 1% (for resident seller) or 20% (for NRI seller) and file Form 26QB within 30 days of payment or property registration, whichever is earlier. This form must be filed online on the income tax portal after paying the TDS amount via challan.
What is Form 26QC and who needs to file it? +
Form 26QC is for TDS deduction on rent payments. If you're a tenant (individual or business) paying rent exceeding ‚Çπ50,000 per month (or ‚Çπ6 lakhs annually) to a resident Indian landlord, you must deduct 10% TDS and file Form 26QC. This is applicable for all types of rent - residential, commercial, or land. The form must be filed quarterly within 30 days of the quarter end. It's not applicable if paying rent to NRI landlords (different provisions apply).
How much does 26QB/26QC filing cost? +
Our Form 26QB filing service costs ‚Çπ2,000 (regular) or ‚Çπ3,000 (same-day urgent service). Form 26QC filing costs ‚Çπ1,500 per quarter or ‚Çπ5,000 for annual package (all 4 quarters). These fees include complete TDS calculation, challan generation support, online form filing, acknowledgement download, and TDS certificate (Form 16B/16C) generation. We also provide correction/revision support if needed.
What happens if I don't file Form 26QB or 26QC on time? +
Late filing attracts heavy penalties: ‚Çπ200 per day late fee (minimum ‚Çπ10,000, maximum equal to TDS amount) plus interest at 1.5% per month on the TDS amount from due date. For property transactions, Sub-Registrar offices may refuse registration without Form 26QB challan. The seller/landlord cannot claim TDS credit in their ITR without proper filing, which may lead to disputes. Criminal prosecution is also possible in severe cases of non-compliance.
How long does the filing process take? +
With all documents ready, we can complete the filing within 24 hours (regular service) or 4-6 hours (urgent same-day service). The process involves: TDS calculation (30 mins), challan payment by you (immediate), form preparation and filing by us (2-3 hours), and TDS certificate download (available immediately after successful filing). For urgent property registration cases, we prioritize your filing to ensure you get the challan copy on the same day.
What documents are required for 26QB filing? +
For Form 26QB, you need: 1) Buyer's PAN and Aadhaar, 2) Seller's PAN and Aadhaar, 3) Property details (address, survey number), 4) Sale deed or agreement copy, 5) Sale consideration amount, 6) Property registration details (if done), 7) TDS challan payment proof with BSR code. If seller is NRI, you'll also need their passport copy and foreign address. We provide a detailed checklist based on your specific transaction.
Can I claim TDS refund if excess TDS was deducted? +
Yes, the seller/landlord can claim TDS refund while filing their income tax return. The TDS deducted appears in their Form 26AS and can be claimed as tax credit. If their total tax liability is less than TDS deducted, they'll get the excess refund after ITR processing. For property sales, if the seller's capital gains tax is lower than 1% TDS deducted, they can claim the balance. We assist in downloading Form 16B/16C which the seller needs to attach with their ITR.
Is 26QB required for property below ‚Çπ50 lakhs? +
No, Form 26QB is not required if the property value is below ‚Çπ50 lakhs. However, this threshold applies to the total sale consideration, not the stamp duty value. If you're buying multiple properties from the same seller in the same financial year and the total exceeds ‚Çπ50 lakhs, TDS is applicable on the combined value. Agricultural land is exempt from TDS regardless of value. For properties eligible for capital gains exemption, you may apply for lower/nil TDS certificate from the Income Tax department.
Do I need to file 26QC if rent is paid in cash? +
Yes, Form 26QC filing is mandatory regardless of payment mode (cash, cheque, or online transfer) if rent exceeds ‚Çπ50,000 per month. You must deduct 10% TDS before making the rent payment and deposit it with the government. However, paying rent in cash above ‚Çπ10,000 in a day or ‚Çπ1 lakh in a year is not allowed under Income Tax rules and can attract penalties. It's always advisable to pay rent via bank transfer for proper record-keeping and TDS compliance.
Can I correct mistakes in already filed 26QB/26QC? +
Yes, you can file a correction statement to rectify errors in Form 26QB or 26QC. Common corrections include: wrong PAN of buyer/seller/landlord, incorrect property address, TDS amount errors, or wrong payment dates. Corrections must be filed through the income tax portal with the original acknowledgement number. We charge ‚Çπ1,000 for correction/revision filing service. It's important to correct errors quickly as wrong PAN or amount can prevent the seller/landlord from claiming TDS credit, leading to disputes and potential legal issues.
