Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost of business tax filing?
Business tax filing starts at ‚Çπ8,000 for presumptive taxation (ITR-4). Proprietorship returns (ITR-3) cost ‚Çπ12,000-20,000. Partnership/LLP returns range from ‚Çπ15,000-25,000. Company tax returns (ITR-6) start at ‚Çπ20,000. Tax audit is additional from ‚Çπ25,000. Pricing depends on turnover, complexity, number of transactions, and whether books audit is required.
Which ITR form should my business file?
ITR-3 for sole proprietorships with regular books of accounts. ITR-4 for small businesses opting for presumptive taxation (turnover ‚â§ ‚Çπ2 crores). ITR-5 for partnerships, LLPs, and AOPs. ITR-6 for all companies (private, public, OPC). The correct form depends on your business structure, turnover, and whether you maintain books of accounts.
What is presumptive taxation under Section 44AD?
Section 44AD is a simplified scheme for small businesses with turnover up to ‚Çπ2 crores. You declare 8% of turnover (6% for digital receipts) as income without maintaining detailed books. Benefits: no books maintenance, simple compliance, lower cost. Drawback: cannot claim actual expenses if higher than 8%. Once opted, must continue for 5 years. Professional limit under 44ADA is ‚Çπ50 lakhs with 50% presumptive income.
When is tax audit mandatory for businesses?
Tax audit under Section 44AB is mandatory if: (1) Business turnover exceeds ‚Çπ1 crore (‚Çπ10 crore if 95% receipts are digital and payments are digital), (2) Professional receipts exceed ‚Çπ50 lakhs, (3) You're claiming income lower than presumptive rates after opting for presumptive taxation. Audit report (Form 3CD/3CB) must be obtained by September 30th and filed by October 31st along with ITR.
What are advance tax payment rules?
If estimated tax liability exceeds ‚Çπ10,000, advance tax must be paid in 4 installments: 15% by June 15, 45% by September 15, 75% by December 15, and 100% by March 15. Failure to pay attracts interest under Section 234B (1% per month on shortfall) and 234C (1% per month on installment shortfall). Senior citizens not having business income are exempt from advance tax.
What is the business tax filing deadline?
For non-audit cases: July 31st. For cases requiring audit: October 31st (extended from September 30th for FY 2023-24). Transfer pricing cases: November 30th. Belated return can be filed till December 31st of the assessment year with late fees. Revised return can be filed within the same timeline if errors are found.
Do I need to maintain books of accounts?
Yes, books maintenance is mandatory if: Business turnover exceeds ‚Çπ25 lakhs, professional receipts exceed ‚Çπ25 lakhs, you're a company/LLP/partnership, or tax audit is applicable. Books must include cash book, ledgers, journal, bank books, invoices, and supporting vouchers. Not required for presumptive taxation (44AD/44ADA). Retain books for 8 years from end of relevant assessment year.
What business expenses can I claim as deductions?
Allowable expenses: Salaries and wages, rent and utilities, raw materials, interest on business loans, depreciation on assets, repairs and maintenance, travel and conveyance, marketing and advertising, professional fees, insurance premiums, telephone and internet. Cannot claim: Personal expenses, capital expenditure, owner's drawings, fines and penalties, CSR expenses (for companies), or expenses not supported by proper documentation.
What is TDS compliance for businesses?
Businesses must deduct TDS on payments exceeding threshold limits: Salary (no limit), contractor payments (‚Çπ30,000), professional fees (‚Çπ30,000), rent (‚Çπ2.4 lakhs annually), commission/brokerage (‚Çπ15,000). Deposit TDS by 7th of next month. File quarterly TDS returns (24Q for salary, 26Q for others). Issue TDS certificates (Form 16/16A). Non-compliance attracts interest, penalty, and disallowance of expenses.

Yes, we provide complete support for: Scrutiny assessment notices, income mismatch notices, defective return notices, demand notices, inquiry proceedings, assessment orders, penalty proceedings, appeals to CIT(A) and ITAT, settlement applications, and rectification requests. We prepare responses, compile documentation, and represent you before tax authorities to resolve matters favorably.
