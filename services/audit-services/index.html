Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost of audit services?
Statutory audit starts at ‚Çπ15,000 for small companies and goes up to ‚Çπ1 lakh+ for large companies. Tax audit ranges from ‚Çπ20,000-50,000. GST audit starts at ‚Çπ30,000. Internal audit costs ‚Çπ25,000-100,000 per quarter. Final pricing depends on company size, turnover, transaction volume, and audit scope.
Which companies require statutory audit?
All Private Limited Companies, Public Limited Companies, One Person Companies (OPC), and Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs) with turnover exceeding ‚Çπ40 lakhs or capital contribution exceeding ‚Çπ25 lakhs require statutory audit as per Companies Act 2013 and LLP Act 2008.
What is the difference between statutory audit and tax audit?
Statutory audit is mandatory under Companies Act for companies to verify financial statements and ensure compliance with accounting standards. Tax audit is required under Section 44AB of Income Tax Act for businesses exceeding specified turnover limits to verify accounts and tax computations. Both serve different regulatory purposes.
When is tax audit required?
Tax audit is required if business turnover exceeds ‚Çπ1 crore (‚Çπ10 crore if 95% receipts are digital and payments are digital), professional receipts exceed ‚Çπ50 lakhs, or when opting out of presumptive taxation. The audit report (Form 3CD/3CB) must be filed by September 30th of the assessment year.
How long does an audit take?
A typical statutory audit takes 2-4 weeks depending on company size, record quality, and complexity. Tax audit takes 1-2 weeks. GST audit requires 1-3 weeks. Internal audit duration varies based on scope. We ensure timely completion to meet all compliance deadlines.
What documents are needed for audit?
Required documents include: Financial statements (P&L, Balance Sheet), Trial Balance, Bank statements, Cash books, Sales & purchase registers, Stock statements, Tax returns (ITR, GST), TDS certificates, Fixed asset register, Loan documents, and supporting vouchers/invoices.
Can you handle audit for multiple locations?
Yes, we conduct audits for companies with multiple branches, warehouses, and locations across India. We have a network of associated CAs in different cities and use technology for efficient multi-location audit coordination and reporting.
What is included in the audit report?
The audit report includes: Auditor's opinion on financial statements, CARO reporting (for companies), observations and qualifications (if any), compliance status, internal control evaluation, and recommendations for improvement. Reports are prepared as per applicable auditing standards.
Do you provide support for audit queries?
Yes, we provide complete support for handling any queries from ROC, Income Tax Department, GST authorities, or banks regarding the audit report. We also assist in responding to statutory notices and compliance-related matters.
When should I appoint an auditor?
For companies, auditors should be appointed within 30 days of incorporation and thereafter at each AGM. For tax audit, it's advisable to appoint auditors by June-July to ensure timely completion before the September 30th deadline. Early appointment ensures smooth audit process.
