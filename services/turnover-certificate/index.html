What Is a Turnover Certificate?

A Turnover Certificate is a formal statement, certified by a Chartered Accountant, that confirms a business's annual sales turnover for one or more financial years — based on audited financials, GST returns, or books of accounts — that certifying authorities can rely on.

A Turnover Certificate states a business's annual sales turnover for one or more financial years, certified against audited financial statements, GST returns (GSTR-1/3B/9), or bank statements. It is signed and stamped by a practicing ICAI-registered CA and is commonly required for government tenders, EMD exemption claims, bank loan applications, and dealership/franchise bids. Common Uses of a Turnover Certificate

Government departments, banks, and tender committees all ask for a CA-certified turnover statement for different reasons. Here are the most common ones.

📄

Government & PSU Tenders

Bidders must submit CA-certified average annual turnover for the last 3-5 years to meet technical/financial eligibility criteria.

💰

EMD & Bid Security Exemption

MSME/Udyam-registered businesses use turnover certificates to support Earnest Money Deposit exemption claims in tenders.

🏦

Bank Loans & Credit Lines

Banks and NBFCs require turnover proof to assess working capital limits, cash credit, and business loan eligibility.

🏢

Dealership & Franchise Bids

Brands require CA-certified turnover figures to qualify prospective dealers, distributors, and franchisees.

📈

Loan Renewal & CMA Data

Turnover certificates support Credit Monitoring Arrangement (CMA) data submissions during loan renewal or enhancement.

⚖️

Legal & Regulatory Filings

Used in arbitration, insolvency proceedings, and regulatory filings requiring verified turnover figures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Direct answers to the most common Turnover Certificate questions

What is a Turnover Certificate?+
A Turnover Certificate is a document certified by a Chartered Accountant that states a business's annual sales turnover for one or more financial years, based on audited financials, GST returns, or books of accounts. It is used for tenders, loans, and EMD exemption claims.
How much does a Turnover Certificate cost?+
A CA-certified Turnover Certificate at Sidhi & Associates starts at ₹2,500 for a single financial year and ₹4,500 for a 3-year average turnover certificate, delivered in 1–3 working days. Call +91 78892 57045.
What documents are required?+
You need audited financial statements or provisional P&L, GST returns (GSTR-1/3B/9), bank statements, PAN, and ITR acknowledgements for the relevant financial years. Our CA confirms the exact list based on your purpose.
Is it mandatory for government tenders?+
Yes, most government and PSU tenders require bidders to submit a CA-certified average annual turnover certificate for the last 3–5 financial years as part of the technical/financial eligibility criteria.
Can a Turnover Certificate be issued for a non-audited business?+
Yes. For businesses below the statutory audit threshold, a CA can certify turnover based on GST returns, provisional accounts, and bank statements, clearly stating the basis of certification on the certificate.
What is a UDIN and why does my certificate need one?+
UDIN (Unique Document Identification Number) is an ICAI-mandated number every practicing CA must generate for certificates they sign. It allows tender authorities and banks to verify the certificate's authenticity online. All our Turnover Certificates carry a valid UDIN.
Can I get this certificate fully online without visiting an office?+
Yes. Share your documents via WhatsApp, email, or our secure Client Portal, and we deliver a signed, UDIN-verified digital certificate — no office visit required, available Pan-India.
