Association of Persons (AOP)

AOP is a group of persons (individuals, companies, firms, or any combination) who join together for a common purpose to earn income. It's not a legal entity but a taxable entity recognized under Income Tax Act.

  • ‚úì Group of any persons

Body of Individuals (BOI)

BOI is specifically a group of individuals (natural persons only, not companies or firms) who come together for earning income. It's similar to AOP but restricted to individuals only.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AOP and BOI?
AOP (Association of Persons) is a group of persons (individuals, companies, firms, or any combination) who join together for a common purpose to earn income. BOI (Body of Individuals) is similar but specifically refers to a group of individuals only (natural persons). Both are recognized taxable entities under the Income Tax Act. Common examples include joint property ownership, clubs, societies, joint ventures, co-ownership of business, and group investments. AOP/BOI is not a legal entity but a tax entity requiring separate PAN card.

What is the difference between AOP and Partnership?
AOP is a tax entity recognized under Income Tax Act formed with simple deed or agreement, suitable for temporary arrangements and joint ventures. Partnership is a legal entity registered under Partnership Act with formal partnership deed, suitable for ongoing businesses. AOP has simpler formation and can include any persons (individuals, companies, firms), while Partnership has only individual partners. AOP can be temporary or project-specific, while Partnership is generally for ongoing business. AOP has lower compliance compared to Partnership. Both have unlimited liability for members/partners.
Is PAN card mandatory for AOP/BOI?
Yes, PAN card is mandatory for AOP/BOI if they have taxable income, need to file income tax returns, or conduct financial transactions. PAN is required for opening bank accounts in AOP/BOI name, GST registration, TDS compliance, and most business activities. We help you obtain PAN card for AOP/BOI by filing Form 49A with Income Tax Department along with AOP/BOI deed and member documents. PAN is usually issued within 15-20 days of application.
How is AOP/BOI taxed?
AOP/BOI is taxed as a separate entity. Tax rate is either 30% (plus surcharge and cess) flat rate, or maximum marginal rate applicable to members if their individual shares are determinate. If income is distributed to members after paying tax at AOP level, members don't pay tax again on that income. If income shares are known and determined in AOP deed, members can include their share in individual ITR and pay tax at their applicable slab rates. Proper tax planning can result in significant tax savings. We provide complete tax advisory for optimal tax structure.
Can AOP/BOI open a bank account?
Yes, AOP/BOI can open a bank account in its name. Required documents include: PAN card of AOP/BOI, AOP/BOI deed/agreement, PAN and Aadhaar of all members, address proof of AOP/BOI registered address, board resolution for authorized signatories, and KYC documents of authorized signatories. Most banks accept AOP/BOI accounts. We provide complete support for bank account opening including documentation and liaison with banks.
Is GST registration mandatory for AOP/BOI?
GST registration is mandatory for AOP/BOI if: aggregate turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (‚Çπ20 lakhs for special category states), engaged in inter-state supply of goods/services, or engaged in certain specified activities (e-commerce, etc.). If turnover is below threshold and no mandatory criteria apply, GST registration is optional. We help with GST registration for AOP/BOI including application filing, documentation, and initial compliance setup.
What documents are needed for AOP/BOI registration?
Required documents include: PAN card of all members, Aadhaar card/ID proof of all members, address proof of all members (utility bill, rental agreement), passport size photographs of all members, email ID and mobile number of all members, proof of registered address for AOP/BOI (if different from members' address), bank statement or utility bill for AOP/BOI address, and details of profit sharing arrangement. We help prepare the AOP/BOI deed/agreement with all necessary clauses as per Income Tax Act requirements.
Can foreigners be members of AOP/BOI?
Yes, foreign nationals and NRIs can be members of AOP/BOI in India. However, additional documentation is required including: valid passport, valid visa (for foreign nationals in India), PAN card (Indian PAN or application for PAN), proof of foreign address, and FEMA compliance if applicable. For certain activities involving foreign members, RBI approval or intimation may be required. We handle all documentation and compliance for AOP/BOI with foreign/NRI members.
How to dissolve an AOP/BOI?
To dissolve an AOP/BOI: Pass dissolution resolution with consent of all members, file final income tax return marking it as final return, close bank accounts, cancel GST registration (if applicable), surrender PAN card and TAN, distribute assets/liabilities among members as per deed, and prepare dissolution deed documenting the closure. Final ITR must be filed within specified time. We provide complete support for AOP/BOI dissolution including all documentation, tax clearances, and final compliance filings.
