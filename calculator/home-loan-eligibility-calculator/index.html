Frequently Asked Questions

How is home loan eligibility calculated? ‚ñº
Home loan eligibility is calculated based on your monthly income, existing EMIs, interest rate, and loan tenure. Banks typically use the Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) of 50-60%. Formula: Maximum EMI = (Net Monthly Income √ó 0.50) - Existing EMIs. The loan amount is then calculated using: Loan Amount = [EMI √ó (1 - (1 + r)^-n)] / r, where r = monthly interest rate and n = tenure in months. Our calculator uses these standard formulas to give you an accurate estimate.
What is the minimum and maximum home loan amount? ‚ñº
Minimum home loan amount varies by bank, typically starting from ‚Çπ1-3 lakhs. Maximum amount depends on your income, credit score, and property value. Most banks offer up to 90% LTV (Loan-to-Value) for properties below ‚Çπ30 lakhs, 80% for ‚Çπ30-75 lakhs, and 75% for higher values. Top earners with excellent credit can get loans up to ‚Çπ5-10 crores. Co-applicants can significantly increase your eligibility. The actual sanctioned amount will be the lower of: calculated eligibility based on income or property value √ó LTV percentage.
How much salary is needed for a home loan? ‚ñº
Minimum salary requirements vary by bank and loan amount. Generally, banks require ‚Çπ25,000-30,000 monthly income for salaried and ‚Çπ4-5 lakhs annual ITR for self-employed. For specific loan amounts: ‚Çπ20L loan needs ~‚Çπ40K salary, ‚Çπ50L needs ~‚Çπ1L salary, ‚Çπ1Cr needs ~‚Çπ2L salary (at 8.5% for 20 years). These are approximate figures assuming no existing EMIs. Higher income, better credit score, or adding a co-applicant can help you qualify for larger loans. Use our calculator with your exact income to get precise eligibility.
Can I get a home loan with a low credit score? ‚ñº
While possible, getting a home loan with a low credit score (below 750) is challenging. Score 650-750: May get loan but at higher interest rates (0.5-2% more) with stricter terms. Score below 650: Most banks reject or require substantial down payment (30-40%) and guarantor. To improve chances: 1) Add co-applicant with good score, 2) Offer higher down payment (30-40%), 3) Show stable income and employment, 4) Provide collateral, 5) Consider smaller loan amount. Better approach: Improve credit score first by paying dues, maintaining low credit utilization, and correcting errors in credit report.
Should I opt for a longer or shorter loan tenure? ‚ñº
Choice depends on your financial goals. LONGER TENURE (25-30 years): Lower EMI, higher eligibility, more tax benefits, but significantly higher total interest. Example: ‚Çπ50L at 8.5% for 30 years = EMI ‚Çπ38,400, total interest ‚Çπ88L. SHORTER TENURE (10-15 years): Higher EMI, lower eligibility, much less interest paid. Same loan for 15 years = EMI ‚Çπ49,200, interest ‚Çπ38L (saves ‚Çπ50L!). Best strategy: Take longer tenure for eligibility but prepay aggressively. This gives flexibility during financial stress while reducing interest through prepayments.
What is LTV ratio and how does it affect my loan? ‚ñº
Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is the loan amount as percentage of property value. RBI guidelines: Properties up to ‚Çπ30L = 90% LTV (10% down payment), ‚Çπ30-75L = 80% LTV (20% down payment), Above ‚Çπ75L = 75% LTV (25% down payment). Higher down payment (lower LTV) means: Better interest rates (0.25-0.5% discount), faster approval, lower EMI burden, more negotiating power. If your eligibility is higher than 75-80% LTV, you'll get loan limited to LTV percentage of property value, not your full eligibility.
Can both husband and wife take a joint home loan? ‚ñº
Yes! Joint home loans offer multiple benefits: 1) HIGHER ELIGIBILITY: Both incomes combined increases loan amount, 2) TAX BENEFITS: Both can claim ‚Çπ2L interest + ‚Çπ1.5L principal deduction each = total ‚Çπ7L deduction, 3) LOWER INTEREST: Some banks offer 0.05% discount for women co-applicants, 4) EASIER APPROVAL: Two income sources reduce risk. Requirements: Both must be co-owners of property, both sign loan agreement, both are equally liable for repayment. Ideal when both are working professionals wanting to maximize loan amount and tax benefits.
What are the tax benefits on home loans? ‚ñº
Section 24(b): Deduct up to ‚Çπ2 lakhs interest per year on home loan (both self-occupied and rented property). Section 80C: Deduct up to ‚Çπ1.5 lakhs principal repayment per year. Section 80EE: Additional ‚Çπ50,000 deduction for first-time buyers (loan up to ‚Çπ35L, property up to ‚Çπ50L). Section 80EEA: Extra ‚Çπ1.5L deduction for affordable housing (stamp duty value up to ‚Çπ45L). Joint loan: Each co-borrower can claim these deductions separately, effectively doubling benefits. Pre-construction interest: Allowed in 5 equal installments after possession. Note: These are available only in old tax regime.
Should I prepay my home loan or invest the money? ‚ñº
Depends on multiple factors: PREPAY IF: 1) Loan interest rate > expected investment returns (8.5% loan vs 7% FD), 2) You're risk-averse, 3) Peace of mind is priority, 4) You've maximized tax-saving investments, 5) Nearing retirement. INVEST IF: 1) Can earn >10-12% returns consistently, 2) Young with long investment horizon, 3) Haven't maxed out EPF/PPF/ELSS, 4) Have emergency fund, 5) Comfortable with debt. BALANCED APPROACH: Do both - invest in equity/mutual funds while making small prepayments annually. Use loan tenure of 20+ years for lower EMI, but target finishing in 12-15 years through prepayments.
What happens if I can't pay EMI due to job loss? ‚ñº
Immediate steps: 1) INFORM BANK: Contact immediately, don't avoid calls, 2) REQUEST MORATORIUM: Some banks offer 3-6 months payment pause (interest still accrues), 3) RESTRUCTURE LOAN: Extend tenure to reduce EMI, switch to interest-only payments temporarily, 4) USE EMI PROTECTION: If you have loan insurance, file claim. Prevention: Maintain 6-12 months EMI as emergency fund, consider EMI protection insurance (costs 0.5-1% of loan), avoid stretching to maximum eligibility. Missing EMIs damages credit score, incurs penalty (2-3% + interest), and risks property attachment after 90+ days default. Always communicate with bank proactively.
